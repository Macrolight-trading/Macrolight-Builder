# Macrolight Builder — 30-Day Content Plan to Boost SEO, AEO & GEO

_Plan window: June 15 – July 10, 2026 (4 weeks) · Cadence: 5 publishes/week + daily distribution · Created June 9, 2026_

---

## 1. The goal in one paragraph

Macrolight Builder sells high-converting, SEO-tuned websites to local service businesses (HVAC, dental, med-spa, law, home services, fitness, contractors) across Birmingham, MI / Oakland County / Metro Detroit, then hosts and improves them on a monthly subscription. This plan makes Macrolight's own site the answer that shows up three ways: ranked in classic Google results (**SEO**), pulled into featured snippets / People Also Ask / voice answers (**AEO**), and cited inside ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews (**GEO**). The lead magnet — the free 50-point SEO audit — is the CTA on every piece.

This is deliberately a double-duty play: the AEO/GEO education content below isn't just how Macrolight gets found, it's proof-of-expertise content that demonstrates the exact service Macrolight sells to clients.

## 2. What SEO, AEO, and GEO each require (the rules every piece follows)

These three overlap but optimize for different surfaces. Every brief in the calendar is tagged with which one it leads on, but **every** post must satisfy the shared baseline.

| Lever | What it wins | What the content must do |
|---|---|---|
| **SEO** | Blue-link rankings, organic traffic | Target a real keyword with intent, match search intent, strong title/H1/meta, internal links, fast page, mobile-clean, indexed in Google **and Bing** |
| **AEO** | Featured snippets, People Also Ask, voice answers | Answer-first format, one question per H2 phrased exactly as users ask it, 40–55 word direct answer immediately under each, **FAQPage JSON-LD**, lists/tables/steps |
| **GEO** | Citations inside ChatGPT / Perplexity / Gemini / AI Overviews | TL;DR in the first 200 words that fully answers the query, claims backed by data/stats, original tables and charts (not stock imagery), visible author + publish + updated dates, comparison framing, entity consistency |

### Shared baseline checklist — applies to every single post

- **TL;DR / answer box** in the first 150–200 words that directly answers the title question. AI retrieval judges relevance on opening content; the first 200 words should stand alone as the answer.
- **One H2 = one question**, worded the way a real owner would type or speak it, with a 40–55 word direct answer right below before you elaborate.
- **FAQPage JSON-LD** with 3–6 Q&A pairs — the single highest-impact schema for AEO/GEO. Google AI Overviews and Perplexity pull these directly.
- **Article + Author schema** (E-E-A-T): real byline (Bradley Bayley / Nick Ottoy), bio, publish date, and updated date visible on-page.
- **One original asset per post**: a data table, a custom chart, a checklist graphic, or a screenshot — never stock photography. AI vision models ignore stock and reward proprietary visuals.
- **At least one statistic with a source** and one comparison or numbered framework — both heavily over-represented in LLM citations.
- **Internal links**: every post links to the free SEO audit, the pricing page, and 2–3 related posts to build topical clusters.
- **Bing Webmaster Tools submission** on publish — ChatGPT Search runs on Bing's index, so Bing indexing is non-negotiable for ChatGPT citations.
- **Primary CTA**: "Get your free 50-point SEO audit." Secondary CTA: see pricing.

### Site-wide technical tasks (do once, in Week 1 — they lift everything)

- Add **LocalBusiness** + **Service** schema to the homepage and every service/location page (NAP, area served, hours).
- Stand up an `/answers` or FAQ hub that aggregates every FAQ block site-wide with FAQPage schema.
- Confirm XML sitemap is submitted to **both** Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools.
- Add a visible "Last updated" date pattern to the blog template.
- Add **Speakable** schema to FAQ answers for voice-assistant eligibility.

## 3. Content pillars & keyword clusters

Six pillars, mapped to buyer intent. The calendar rotates through them so no single cluster cannibalizes itself.

1. **Local geo-targeting** (SEO lead) — "[service] web design [city]". Birmingham, Royal Oak, Troy, Bloomfield Hills, Ferndale, Rochester, Novi, Ann Arbor. Highest commercial intent; these convert.
2. **Vertical web design** (SEO + GEO) — "web design for [HVAC / dentists / med-spas / law firms / contractors / gyms]". Owners search by their own industry.
3. **SEO/AEO/GEO education** (AEO + GEO lead) — definitional, "what is / how do I" content that gets cited and demonstrates expertise. Doubles as sales proof.
4. **Comparison / "vs"** (GEO lead) — "Wix vs custom site," "agency vs freelancer," "DIY vs done-for-you." LLMs cite comparison content disproportionately; these also catch bottom-funnel buyers.
5. **ROI & proof** (AEO + SEO) — cost, ROI, "is it worth it," timelines, case studies. Handles objections.
6. **Programmatic location × service pages** (SEO at scale) — `/services/[service]/[city]` matrix from the build plan. Batch-published, lightweight, schema-rich.

**Cannibalization guardrail** — these existing posts already own their topics; new pieces must link to, not compete with, them:
- _5 Website Mistakes Costing Contractors Leads Every Day_
- _How a $200/mo Website Generates $20K in Monthly Revenue_
- _How Long Does a New Local Business Website Take to Rank on Google?_
- _Web Design in Birmingham, MI_
- _Why Your Local Business Needs More Than a Digital Business Card_

## 5. Distribution & repurposing (do daily — 80% of the leverage is here)

Publishing is half the job; distribution and brand-signal building is what gets you cited. For each post:

- **LinkedIn** — Bradley posts a 5–7 sentence takeaway with the post's key stat or table image, day of publish. Founder-led posts compound brand entity signals that LLMs weight.
- **Google Business Profile post** — push every location and ROI piece to GBP; 58% of voice searches are local-intent and GBP feeds them.
- **Repurpose the TL;DR + FAQ** into a short LinkedIn carousel or X thread once per week.
- **Email** — one digest per week to the list with the two strongest pieces.
- **Brand-signal outreach (weekly)** — pitch one guest byline or get one mention on a Metro Detroit small-business / industry publication. Identify 5–10 trusted outlets in month 1; relationship-building, not blast PR. This is the single biggest GEO lever outside your own site.
- **Internal linking pass (Fridays)** — link the week's new posts into the relevant existing cluster and the cornerstone audit post.

## 6. How to measure it (review weekly, score at day 30)

**SEO**
- Impressions & clicks per target keyword (Google Search Console + **Bing Webmaster Tools**)
- Keyword rankings for the 20 primary terms
- Indexed page count (esp. programmatic batches)

**AEO**
- Featured-snippet / People Also Ask captures for target questions
- FAQ-rich-result eligibility in Search Console

**GEO**
- Citations / mentions in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, AI Overviews — spot-check each target query weekly; log which engine cited you
- Referral traffic from `chatgpt.com`, `perplexity.ai`, `gemini.google.com` in analytics (LLM referrals convert far above organic — ~16% from ChatGPT vs ~1.8% typical organic, so even small volume matters)

**Business outcome (the one that counts)**
- Free SEO-audit requests attributed to content
- Audit → discovery-call → subscription conversion

> **Reality check on timeline:** classic SEO rankings take 30–180 days to mature (you already publish this honestly in your "how long to rank" post). AEO and GEO move faster — well-structured FAQ and comparison content can get pulled into snippets and AI answers within weeks of indexing. Expect AEO/GEO wins first, organic ranking compounding after.

- Every vertical (HVAC, dental, med-spa, law, contractors, fitness) and every target city maps to a real ICP segment and to the programmatic `/services/[service]/[city]` engine already in the build plan — so the calendar feeds the product, not just the blog.

- Comparison pieces ("Wix vs custom," "agency vs freelancer," "DIY vs done-for-you") catch buyers at the decision moment and are the format LLMs cite most.
- No piece overlaps the five posts you've already published; each new cluster links back to them instead.
